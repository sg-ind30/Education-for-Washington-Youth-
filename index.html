Our Mission

Education for Washington Youth (EWY) is a nonprofit dedicated to closing opportunity gaps by connecting underserved Kâ€“12 students in Washington State with University of Washington (UW) student mentors. Through our Science Olympiad, Math Olympiad, VEX Robotics, and academic mentorship programs, we aim to develop confident, skilled, and motivated young leaders in STEM and other academic fields.

Our Programs

EWY offers structured mentorship programs led by trained UW student volunteers. Each program is designed to provide targeted guidance, educational resources, and role-model support.

Science Olympiad Mentorship

University of Washington students with experience in Science Olympiad provide weekly mentorship to middle and high school teams.

  • Weekly subject-specific coaching sessions
  • Access to past exams and study guides
  • Mock testing events and regional prep camps

Math Olympiad Mentorship

UW students assist students preparing for Math Olympiad contests such as AMC 8/10, MathCounts, and school-based math leagues.

  • Tiered instruction by grade level
  • Weekly problem-solving sessions
  • Math strategy bootcamps and competition simulations

Robotics Mentorship (VEX IQ)

EWY helps students get involved in VEX IQ Robotics by providing engineering, design, and coding mentorship.

  • Robot design and build support
  • Programming workshops using VEXcode
  • Team organization and competition prep

UW Student Mentorship

This unique program matches students with UW mentors based on their future academic or career interests.

  • Day-in-the-life mentor shadows (virtual or in-person)
  • Career-aligned mentorship in CS, Pre-Med, Engineering, etc.
  • Coding, anatomy, and study skills camps
  • Resume-building workshops and productivity systems

Program Guidelines

Guidelines for Mentees

  • Be respectful, prepared, and open to learning
  • Attend all scheduled sessions and communicate absences early
  • Complete assigned tasks and actively participate

Guidelines for Mentors

  • Commit to consistent, prepared mentorship
  • Tailor sessions to mentee goals and academic level
  • Report progress and issues to EWY leadership
  • Serve as a role model and source of inspiration
